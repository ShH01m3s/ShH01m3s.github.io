When something keeps returning

You may find yourself reacting more strongly than the situation seems to call for. The same conflicts return. Small things begin to feel charged. You may feel misunderstood by others — or unclear, at times, even to yourself. These experiences are rarely meaningless. They may be signs that something beneath conscious awareness is trying to make itself known. This is where the work begins.

  • What to expect

What to expect

We begin by talking. You bring what is on your mind — I listen for what may be moving underneath it: the repetitions, defences, conflicts, fears, and desires shaping your present life. This work can happen through conversation and the therapeutic relationship. Sometimes clinical hypnotherapy might be a better way forward — approaching the same material through attention, imagery, bodily experience, and altered states of awareness.

About me

My work is shaped by psychodynamic and depth-psychological traditions, including Freud, Jung, Klein, Bowlby, and Kohut. I am interested in the unconscious life of the person: the attachments, defences, losses, images, and repetitions that shape how we suffer and how we relate. I trained in both psychodynamic counselling and clinical hypnotherapy because I see them as complementary ways of working with unconscious material — through language, relationship, imagery, sensation, attention, and suggestion.
