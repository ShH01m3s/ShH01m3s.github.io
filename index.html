About me

Imagine a naughty cat has scratched your finger. You can just take a plaster and cover the damaged area, wagging a finger and exerting all of your strength to give a stern stare. We all know what good that will do, however, but *hope springs eternal*. Now imagine that you have a slightly more serious scratch on the back and it's challenging to even put a plaster over it all by yourself. It aches, it itches, you can't sleep. You ask a friend to help, apply some medically-smelling cream all over it and happy with the placebo effect it has. But the next time that same friend is unavailable and you decide to take this problem to the nearest medical facility. Some young and unexperienced intern is smiling it you, assessing your injury under the swirling gaze of a medical supervisor. At the end, you get the help you needed, heading home happily and ready for the useless figer-wagging again. You start sharing your concerns about the cat being so unreasonable and naughty all the time, but the friend is busy with his affairs and can barely spend 30 mins listening, let alone, helping you. He suggests to put the cat down for good, and you are horrified but that proposal, but the next day, with the next scratch you start thinking about other methods to try. What if I talk to the vet, you think.

Interests

My main interests are psychodynamic therapy and hypnosis. That's not to say I'm not open to different tools and ideas from other approaches (CBT, SFT etc). Quite on a contrary: we know too little and we are too different. A lot remains hidden from our keen eye in the field of psychology.
