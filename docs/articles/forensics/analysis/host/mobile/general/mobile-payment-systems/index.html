Was originally called GoogleWallet and was just a P2P payment system, when a person could send money to another person. Something like Yandex.Wallet? Then Android Pay emerged and they merged the both into GooglePay.

ApplePay vs GooglePay

Apple üçè Google üåà

Both AP and GP don’t reveal the card details to the PoS terminal. Since attacks on PoS terminals are quite frequent and very costy, it’s a good thing. Probably, all PoS terminal will stop processing card details some time in future.

GooglePay Wallets stores card details, AppleWallet doesn’t. ApplePay only uses the token that was issued by the Bank (DAN).

Apple Pay claims to be “your wallet without the wallet,” a description that, in truth, fits Google Wallet better. And Google Wallet calls itself “an easier way to pay,” which in all fairness is what Apple’s ought to be saying [1].
