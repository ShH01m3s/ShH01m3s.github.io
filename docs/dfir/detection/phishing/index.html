🎣 Phishing

domain: ir

doctype: article


Phishing, aka brand spoofing, aka carding - various scam that primarily use e-mail messages to trick a person into divulging personal information. This term was first use by hackers when they stole AOL accs.

Spear phishing - targeted attack to a specific users or groups of users.

Whaling - spear phishing for high executive managers.

The most common strategies:

  1. They claim that they’ve seen numerous failed login attempts or other suspicious activity.
  2. They claim that there is some problem with your account or payment method.
  3. Confirm some personal information.
  4. Include a fake invoice.
  5. Click on a link to make a payment.
  6. You are eligible for a government refund.
  7. Coupon for free stuff.

They often use spoofed domains or domains with wrong characters that look like a legit resource. Google, YouTube and Apple are the most spoofed domains. Attackers have started using HTTPS to make their resources look more legitimate.

How to spot?

  • Suspicious sender’s address.
  • Generic signature and greetings.
  • Spoofed hyperlinks and websites.
  • Spelling and layout.
  • Suspicious attachment.

Impact

Some time ago these were used to steal passwords and usernames. But now APTs use this technique, stealing organizations' information and people identities. The average cost of a data breach is $3.86m and phishing accouts for 90% of them. 15% of people who fell the victims to a phishing scam, will be scamed at least once more during the year. Around 1.5m phising websites are created each month. 30% of phishing emails get opened.
